Jay’s Sandbox

Jay’s Sandbox is where I park experiments and half-finished ideas—usually maps, small tools, or visualizations that help me think through wind, land, and data. Nothing here is tied to a specific real-world project or employer. It’s all demos, dummy data, and explorations I’m comfortable sharing publicly.

Current highlight · Webmap experiment

Terrain + Access: Playing with Road Concepts

This demo map uses public elevation and landcover data to explore how basic slope and access assumptions start to shape a conceptual wind project footprint. It’s not a real site—just a sandbox—but the idea is the same: roads want to thread along friendlier ground while still reaching logical turbine pads.

Why I built it: I wanted a shareable way to explain why “drive straight there” almost never works once you look at terrain and drainage.

What I’m testing: Simple slope thresholds, a rough “avoid this” layer, and a few options for tying into existing roads.
